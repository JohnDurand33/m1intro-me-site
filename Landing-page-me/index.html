Not so long ago I worked in operations management for a telecommunications company.

I traveled the world and managed an army of people across three continents...

The places and people were amazing

We stood up sites in foreign countries, trained the staff on how to operate, and worked hard, together. We saw some amazing sights! Asia, Africa, Central America. Seven countries in total, seven sites. All places I never would have dreamed I'd see, let alone spend time in. I made long lasting relationships over the 10 years in this role, and I had the opportunity to build it! Who put me in charge?... Thank you btw, it was an adventure to say the least!

My team was the BEST

For 10 years my team members came, worked hard, had fun, and left. But this group was the cream of the crop. When we were together, we could accomplish anything, and did I mention we had fun? Look at all these ridiculous images AI built of them lol.

Sean was always happy-go-lucky and had something smart to say, all you had to do was ask (or not, sometimes). Chi was the calm, quiet one, but when he spoke people listened. The guy could do so many things at once, it was amazing. Wahid: the absolute hardest worker and kindest individual I've had the pleasure to know. And Clay, well Clay was probably the smartest person on the planet. Sometimes I felt like we were the Avengers.

And that was what I did ALL the time.

But, as years went on, the job became more time-consuming.

The love of my life became more difficult to spend time with. In fact, let me tell you about Diane..

I thought "Soulmate" was just something people say..

We moved in together after 2 weeks and never looked back.

Diane and I met online in 2017. If you didn't know, meeting people at 35 when work owns your life isn't as easy as you might expect, lol. But, we met! And we went to brunch on Mother's Day. And it lasted 5 hours. And if we didn't both have plans it might have lasted longer. I traveled to the Domician Republic the next week, and thought about her the entire time.

I had a work trip to New York the following week, and our date had gone so well, I invited her to come... and she said yes! It was there we fell in LOVE (cheesy I know), but not one day goes by I'm not thankful for her. Last year we got a Boxer, "Nola", and I wasn't even there to help raise her I was gone so much. Which brings me to why I'm here. Work can be amazing, but at some point you have to choose. And for me, the decision was easy. I chose the love of my life (and Nola). Now, I'm happier than I've ever been, and excited to enter this new chapter of my life.
